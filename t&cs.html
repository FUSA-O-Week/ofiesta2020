PROMOTER

Flinders University (“Promoter” “we”, “us” or “our”) is conducting the O’Fiesta Event (“Event”).

When you purchase your ticket to attend the Event you agree to comply with these Terms and Conditions.

The Promoter has engaged security and other staff (“Event staff”) to ensure that the Event is appropriately and safely conducted on behalf of the Promoter and has authorised Event staff to exercise the rights and powers described in these Terms and Conditions.

PROOF OF AGE

The Event is a strictly 18+ event. You must have valid proof of age photo ID to enter the event. Persons under the age of 18 years will be denied entry. The Promoter reserves the right to evict any underage person (or persons unable to provide valid ID). No refunds will be processed for anyone who is denied entry because they fail to provide Accepted ID.

Accepted ID will be as follows:

  • Australian Photo Driver’s Licence
  • Proof of Age Card
  • Keypass Photo ID Card (Australia Post)
  • Passport (must be current and age clearly identifiable

EVENT ENTRY

Entry to the Event will be granted with presentation of a ticket issued by an Authorised Ticketing Agency (Moshtix or FUSA) in the following forms:

  • a valid bar-coded ticket
  • a valid bar-coded print-at-home PDF ticket
  • a valid bar-coded electronic ticket on mobile device

SEARCH ON ENTRY

It is a condition of entry to the Event Site that our Event staff can search you and/or your possessions at the time of your entry to the Event Site. If you do not consent to such searches, you may be denied entry to the Event Site without refund.

You must not bring Prohibited items into the Event Site. Prohibited Items include but are not limited to the list of items at the end of these Event Terms & Conditions and any Prohibited Items which we find will be confiscated. Confiscated items will not be returned, replaced or reimbursed. Event staff have the right to refuse entry to any persons found to be carrying Prohibited Items.

DIRECTIONS OF EVENT STAFF

You must follow the reasonable directions of Event staff as required and comply with the law at all times during the Event.

BEHAVIOUR

You must ensure that you act in an orderly manner when attending the Event Site. Event staff may refuse you entry to the Event or evict you from the Event if you are behaving in a disorderly, offensive or inappropriate manner.

NO PASS OUTS

There are no Pass Outs on the day. If you choose to leave the Event at any point, you will not be admitted back in.

SMOKING POLICY

Flinders University has a campus wide No Smoking policy and you must comply with that policy. Event staff have the authority to enforce this policy during the Event.

ALCOHOL

The Event is fully licensed – please do not bring alcohol with you as it will be confiscated and will not be returned. Only alcohol purchased from bars at the Event may be consumed at the Event Site. If you wish to purchase or consume alcohol, you must have valid Accepted ID. You must be of legal age (18 years) to purchase and consume alcohol on the Event Site. If you are found supplying to or buying alcohol for a person under 18 years of age you will be evicted from the Event and may be referred to police.

DRUGS

If you are found supplying, consuming or in possession of illicit drugs you will be evicted from the Event and from the Event Site and may be referred to police.

FOOD AND DRINK

No food or drink will be permitted into the Event Site. The Event is licensed to sell food & drinks and provides great, well-stocked bars. Unsealed EMPTY plastic water bottles (no glass) can be brought into the Event Site. There will be plenty of water filling stations on site.

RISK

Entry to the Event Site is at your own risk. Without limiting the generality of this: appropriate footwear and clothing must be worn at all times at the Event Site; you must not mosh, crowd surf or stage dive: and ear plugs should be worn.

RECORDING AND PHOTOGRAPHY OF THE EVENT

You consent to be included in official photography, film and sound recordings of the Event and for these recordings to be used by Promoter and/or any of its authorised representatives and contractors in any medium or context without further authorisation by, or compensation to, you.

REFUND AND CANCELLATION POLICY

The Event is an all-weather event. The Event will take place rain, hail or shine.

Promoter reserves the right to change, add, withdraw or substitute artists in the Event line-up, vary the advertised program, vary the date and/or vary the Event Site without prior notification.

No ticket refunds or exchanges will be available on ticket purchases except where required by law or in accordance with Live Performance Australia’s Code of Practice for Ticketing of Live Entertainment Events in Australia.

There will be no refund of any unused portion of an Event Ticket if you or one of the people attending the Event with you are evicted from the Event or otherwise required or forced to leave the Event for any reason or if you decide not to attend the Event. You are responsible for checking the age minimum for the Event. No refunds will be given if you are denied entry due to not having Accepted ID for admittance to the Event.

TICKET RESTRICTIONS

You agree not to re-sell Event tickets contrary to applicable laws. Event tickets may not, without the prior written consent of Promoter:

  • be re-sold or offered for resale at a premium (including via online auction sites); or
  • used for advertising, promotion or other commercial purposes (including competitions and trade promotions); or
  • used to enhance the demand for other goods or services, either by the original purchaser or any subsequent bearer.

If an Event ticket is used in breach of this condition, the Event ticket may be cancelled without a refund and the bearer of the Event ticket may be refused admission. The Promoter will use reasonable efforts to notify you of such action.

TICKET PURCHASE LIMITS

The Promoter reserves the right to enforce published ticket purchase limits and cancel any tickets obtained above said limits in any one transaction or by any one customer. If you exceed the stated ticket limit, you may have all or any of your order and tickets cancelled without notice by Promoter in its discretion. This includes orders associated with the same name, email address, billing address, credit card number or other information.

YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION

Pursuant to your booking, Promoter may need to collect and keep personal information about you including your name, address, credit card or payment details, telephone number and the names of all ticket allocations. Unless you tell us otherwise, Promoter may disclose your contact details to third parties if you elect to receive such information at time of ticket purchase so they can provide you with promotional material about other events and products. Promoter may also need to disclose your personal information as required by law or as permitted under its Privacy Policy. If you have any questions about how Promoter will handle your personal information, please visit the link at the bottom of the Authorised Ticketing Agency’s (Moshtix) ticketing page where you purchased your Event ticket or contact the Promoter.

PROHIBITED ITEMS

The following items are prohibited:

  • Glass
  • Cans
  • Skateboards, scooters, rollerblades, bicycles
  • Chairs (folding, portable, camping) or any other furniture
  • Anything studded or spiked (belts, wristbands, etc) or large chains
  • Weapons of any kind (including potential missiles)
  • Flares, Fireworks or Sparklers
  • Alcohol
  • Illicit Drugs
  • Sound or video recorders or professional cameras (with removable lens) (small still cameras are allowed)
  • Laser lights or laser pens
  • Umbrellas (including small, fold up umbrellas - bring a plastic poncho or raincoat instead)
  • Water pistols
  • Dogs or other pets (service dogs excepted)
  • Fires and open flames of any kind
  • Sound Systems
  • Eskies, coolers or chilly bins
  • Advanced personal vaporizers (or modified APVs) or liquid refills for E-Cigs
  • Drones or other remote control
  • Unauthorised solicitation materials including handbills, flyers, stickers, beach balls, give-aways, samples, or other promotional
  • Any other items deemed dangerous or potentially disruptive to the Event in the discretion of Event Staff
